What is a Repeater?

In ham radio, a repeater is a device that listens for, and then rebroadcasts (repeats) a radio signal with the intent to extend the range of radio communications.

Conseptualy, a repeater, in its most basic form, is very simple and typicaly consists of the folowing componets:

  • Power supply
  • Recieve radio (RX Radio)
  • Transmit Radio (TX radio)
  • A Duplexer
  • An Antenna
  • A Repeater Controler

The KI5TLZ Repeater has all of these things. For our RX Radio, I chose to use an old Motorola GM300 commercial radio. For our TX Radio, I am using a Motorola CDM 1550LS+ commercial radio. The TX radio is transmitting 10 watts. The duplexer was found on Facebook Marketplace. Our antenna is a homebrew 1/4 wave vertical (as of August 2025). Our repeater controller is a Raspberry Pi 4 and a Masters Communications RA-25 sound card running AllstarLink 3.
